Artiklar - Finans/Ekonomi

Optimal vs naive investing
Evaluation of the out-of-sample performance of the sample-based mean-variance model,and its extensions designed to reduce estimation error, relative to the naive 1/N portfolio. Of the 14 models evaluated across seven empirical datasets, none is consistently better than the 1/N rule in terms of Sharpe ratio, certainty-equivalent return, or turnover, which indicates that, out of sample, the gain from optimal diversiﬁcation is more than offset by estimation error.
Discussion of techniques for discounting pension liabilities
Determining Discount Rates Required to Fund Defined Benefit Plans. Model that focuses on the probability that the assets based on current contributions will be less than the value of that liability at some point in the future, requiring further contributions.
Estimating long-run stock market returns (supply side perspective)
We estimate the forward-looking long-term equity risk premium using a combination of the historical and the supply side approaches. We decompose the 1926-2000 historical equity returns into supply factors including inflation, earnings, dividends, price to earnings ratio, dividend payout ratio, book value, return on equity, and GDP per capita.
Moodys default model for private firms
Moody’s RiskCalc™ for private companies uses five broad categories of risk: cash flow/profitability, capital structure, liquidity, activity, and size. This article provides a description of these ratios and explains how they are calculated, and how they are related to default behavior.
Modeling risk premiums in equity market
Estimation of the objective forward-looking U.S. equity risk premium relative to bonds based on an expected real stock return and an expected real bond return. Expected real bond return is based on yields and estimate of expected inflation. Expected real stock return is based on stock dividend yields and an estimate of expected real dividend growth.
